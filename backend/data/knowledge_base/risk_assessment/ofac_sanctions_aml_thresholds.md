# OFAC Sanctions, AML Thresholds, and Regulatory Reporting

> Sources: OFAC/Treasury.gov (FAQs, Topic 1501), FinCEN.gov (BSA Overview, Filing Information), IRS.gov (Publication 550)

## OFAC Sanctions Program

OFAC administers economic and trade sanctions under Presidential national emergency powers and specific legislation. Sanctions can be comprehensive (entire country) or targeted (specific individuals/entities on the SDN List).

### The SDN List

The Specially Designated Nationals and Blocked Persons List includes individuals and entities associated with targeted countries, terrorism, narcotics trafficking, and proliferation.

### The 50 Percent Rule

Any entity owned 50% or more by one or more blocked persons is itself blocked, even if not on the SDN List. Institutions must aggregate ownership interests.

### Blocking and Rejecting

Blocked funds must be held in segregated interest-bearing accounts and reported to OFAC within 10 business days. Prohibited but non-blockable transactions are rejected (not processed).

### Licensing

General licenses authorize classes of transactions automatically. Specific licenses are case-by-case upon written application.

### Penalties

Civil penalties: millions per violation (inflation-adjusted). Criminal: up to $20M and 30 years imprisonment per willful violation.

## AML Reporting Thresholds

### CTR Threshold

File CTR (Form 112) for each cash transaction exceeding $10,000 in a single business day. Multiple transactions totaling over $10,000 by the same person in one day are treated as a single transaction.

### Structuring (31 U.S.C. § 5324)

Federal crime to break up transactions to avoid CTR thresholds. Examples: multiple deposits under $10,000, using multiple branches, instructing others to conduct structured transactions.

### SAR Filing Thresholds

- Banks/depository institutions: $5,000 or more in suspicious transactions
- Money services businesses: $2,000 or more
- Insider abuse: regardless of amount

### FBAR (Form 114)

U.S. persons must file when aggregate value of all foreign financial accounts exceeds $10,000 at any time during the calendar year.

### Form 8300

Businesses must file when receiving more than $10,000 in cash in a single transaction or related transactions.

### CMIR (Form 105)

Required for physical transport of currency or monetary instruments exceeding $10,000 into or out of the United States.

## IRS Wash Sale Rule (Publication 550)

A wash sale occurs when you sell securities at a loss and within 30 days before or after the sale you buy substantially identical securities. The disallowed loss is added to the cost basis of the replacement securities. Also applies to spouse or controlled corporation purchases.

### Capital Gains Tax Rates

| Rate | Applies To |
|------|-----------|
| 0% | Taxpayers in 10% or 12% brackets |
| 15% | Most taxpayers |
| 20% | Taxpayers in 37% bracket |
| 25% | Unrecaptured Section 1250 gain |
| 28% | Collectibles and Section 1202 stock |

Capital loss deduction limit: $3,000/year ($1,500 married filing separately). Unused losses carry forward indefinitely.

## Section 1256 Contracts (Mark-to-Market)

Regulated futures, foreign currency contracts, nonequity options follow the 60/40 rule: 60% long-term and 40% short-term capital gains/losses regardless of holding period.

## Hedging Transactions

Transactions in the normal course of business primarily to manage price or currency risk. Gains and losses from qualifying hedging transactions are treated as ordinary gains and losses.
