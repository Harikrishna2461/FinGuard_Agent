# Fraud Typologies and Red Flags

> Sources: FinCEN.gov (Advisories, Alerts, SAR Key Terms), Investopedia (Suspicious Activity Reports, SAR Filing Triggers)

## Major Fraud Typologies

### 1. Money Laundering

The process of making illegally obtained money appear legitimate. Three stages:

- **Placement**: Introducing illicit funds into the financial system (cash deposits, purchases of monetary instruments, wire transfers)
- **Layering**: Separating funds from their source through complex transactions (wire transfers between accounts, shell company transactions, currency exchanges)
- **Integration**: Returning laundered money to the legitimate economy (real estate purchases, business investments, luxury goods)

### 2. Structuring (Smurfing)

Breaking transactions into smaller amounts to avoid Currency Transaction Report (CTR) thresholds:
- Multiple cash deposits slightly below $10,000
- Using multiple branches or financial institutions
- Directing others to make deposits on one's behalf
- Federal crime under 31 U.S.C. § 5324

### 3. Identity Theft and Fraud

Per FinCEN Notice FIN-2024-NTC1 (April 2024): Use of counterfeit U.S. passport cards to perpetrate identity theft and fraud schemes at financial institutions. Red flags include:
- New accounts opened with recently issued identification
- Multiple accounts opened in rapid succession
- Address mismatches between identification and account application
- Synthetic identity creation (combining real and fabricated information)

### 4. Business Email Compromise (BEC)

Per FinCEN Advisory FIN-2019-A005: Sophisticated schemes where attackers impersonate executives, vendors, or attorneys to redirect wire transfers. SAR Key Terms: BEC FRAUD, EAC FRAUD, BEC DATA THEFT.

Red flags:
- Last-minute changes to wire transfer instructions
- Requests to send funds to previously unknown accounts
- Urgency or pressure in payment requests
- Email addresses that closely mimic legitimate ones

### 5. Deepfake Media Fraud

Per FinCEN Alert FIN-2024-Alert004 (November 2024): Fraud schemes using AI-generated synthetic media (deepfakes) to target financial institutions. SAR Key Term: FIN-2024-DEEPFAKEFRAUD.

Applications:
- Synthetic voice to impersonate executives for wire transfer approvals
- Deepfake video for remote identity verification bypass
- AI-generated documents for loan or account applications

### 6. Virtual Currency Investment Scams ("Pig Butchering")

Per FinCEN Alert FIN-2023-Alert005 (September 2023): Victims are groomed online through social media or dating apps, built trust over weeks, then directed to invest in fraudulent cryptocurrency platforms. SAR Key Term: FIN-2023-PIGBUTCHERING.

Red flags:
- Customer reports being directed to invest by an online acquaintance
- Funds sent to cryptocurrency exchanges or peer-to-peer platforms
- Customer unable to withdraw funds from investment platform
- Increasing amounts transferred over time

### 7. Check Fraud / Mail Theft

Per FinCEN Alert FIN-2023-Alert003 (February 2023): Nationwide surge in mail theft-related check fraud. SAR Key Term: FIN-2023-MAILTHEFT.

Methods:
- Physical theft of checks from U.S. mailboxes
- Chemical washing to alter payee and amount
- Mobile deposit of altered or counterfeit checks
- Sale of stolen checks on dark web markets

### 8. Account Takeover

Per FinCEN Advisory FIN-2011-A016: Unauthorized access to customer accounts. SAR Key Term: ACCOUNT TAKEOVER FRAUD.

Indicators:
- Unauthorized changes to account contact information
- Access from unusual locations or devices
- Rapid fund movements immediately after login
- Multiple failed authentication attempts followed by success

### 9. Ransomware Financial Activity

Per FinCEN Advisory FIN-2021-A004: Financial activity associated with ransomware payments. SAR Key Term: CYBER FIN-2021-A004.

Indicators:
- Sudden large cryptocurrency purchases by entities not typically involved in CVC
- Wire transfers to exchanges shortly after a reported cyber incident
- Payments to CVC addresses known to be associated with ransomware

### 10. Elder Financial Exploitation

Per FinCEN Advisory FIN-2022-A002: Financial abuse targeting elderly individuals. SAR Key Term: EFE FIN-2022-A002.

Red flags:
- Unusual or sudden changes in banking patterns
- Unexplained large withdrawals or transfers
- New individuals added as authorized signers or beneficiaries
- Fear or confusion exhibited during transactions
- Transactions inconsistent with the customer's known financial profile

## Transaction Monitoring Red Flags

### Velocity-Based Indicators
- Rapid succession of transactions in short time windows
- Significant increase in transaction volume over baseline
- Multiple transactions just below reporting thresholds
- High-frequency small deposits followed by single large withdrawal

### Pattern-Based Indicators
- Round-dollar transactions (especially cash)
- Funds flowing through multiple accounts rapidly (layering)
- In-and-out transactions (funds deposited and immediately withdrawn)
- Mirror transactions across multiple institutions
- Cyclical patterns suggesting automated laundering

### Geographic Risk Indicators
- Transactions involving FATF-identified high-risk jurisdictions
- Unexplained cross-border wire transfers
- Mismatches between customer location and transaction origin
- Activity involving jurisdictions with bank secrecy or low transparency

### Customer Behavior Indicators
- Reluctance to provide required identification
- Unusual knowledge of BSA/AML reporting requirements
- Attempts to discourage filing of required reports
- Nervousness or urgency during large cash transactions
- Providing inconsistent or contradictory information
